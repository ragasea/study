Mijbil the Otter — Comprehensive Notes

Chapter: "Mijbil the Otter" — Source: First Flight (Class 10, NCERT)

About the Author

Gavin Maxwell (1914–1969) was a Scottish naturalist and writer. He is best known for Ring of Bright Water, a book describing his life with otters in Scotland. Maxwell had a deep affection for animals and wrote from his personal experiences.

Summary

This chapter is an extract from Ring of Bright Water. The author recounts his experience of keeping an otter as a pet during his stay in Iraq (Basra) and later bringing it to England. The otter—named Mijbil (often called Mij)—becomes a close companion, showing intelligence, playfulness, and a strong love for water.

First Encounter

  • The author originally considered a dog but was offered an otter instead.
  • Mijbil arrived as a shy baby, hiding in a box, but gradually explored and bonded with the author.

Mijbil’s Nature

  • Intelligent, curious, mischievous, and affectionate.
  • Had a natural instinct for swimming and loved water more than land.
  • Invented playful games (marbles, rubber balls, broken suitcases) and displayed quick learning.

The Journey to England

  • Bringing Mijbil to England presented practical problems (air travel restrictions for exotic pets).
  • Mijbil created a commotion at the airport and on the plane but charmed many people with his antics.

Life in London

  • Mijbil attracted attention on walks—people often misidentified him (seal, beaver, squirrel, even walrus!).
  • Otters were rare as pets, so Mijbil was a constant source of fascination.

Themes

  • Man and Nature: The bond between humans and animals and how compassion bridges the gap.
  • Friendship and Love: Care and patience can create deep friendships even with wild creatures.
  • Adaptability: Mijbil adapts from a wild environment to living closely with humans.

Characters

  • Gavin Maxwell — the author and naturalist, patient and caring toward animals.
  • Mijbil (Mij) — the otter: playful, intelligent, mischievous, and affectionate.
  • Air Hostess — an example of the sympathetic people Mijbil meets during travel.

Important Points

  • Mijbil belonged to a breed of otters Maxwell later studied closely.
  • The chapter highlights otters' natural love for water and playful intelligence.
  • Shows that wild animals, given care and kindness, can form bonds with humans.

Moral / Message

Animals deserve love, care, and respect. Friendship and trust between humans and animals are possible through compassion and patience. Nature holds joy and wonder if we choose to connect with it.
