üî¨ Basic Concepts

    [span_0](start_span)
  • **Chemical Reaction**: The transformation of a chemical substance into another chemical substance (e.g., Rusting of iron, the setting of milk into curd).[span_0](end_span)
    • [span_1](start_span)
  • **Chemical Equation**: Representation of a chemical reaction using symbols and formulae of the substances.[span_1](end_span) $$A + B \text{(Reactant)} \rightarrow C + D \text{(Product)}$$ [span_2](start_span)Example: $\text{Mg} + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{MgO}$ (Reactant $\rightarrow$ Product)[span_2](end_span)
    • [span_3](start_span)
  • **Balanced Chemical Equation**: Number of atoms of each element in reactants = number of atoms of each element in products.[span_3](end_span)
    • [span_4](start_span)
  • **Law of Conservation of Mass**: Mass of reactants = Mass of products.[span_4](end_span)

‚öõÔ∏è Types of Chemical Reactions

1. Combination Reaction: $A + B \rightarrow AB$

[span_15](start_span)

Two or more reactants combine to form a single product.[span_15](end_span)

  • Example: $\text{CaO} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{Ca}(\text{OH})_2$
  • Example: $2\text{H}_2 + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{H}_2\text{O}$

2. Decomposition Reaction: $AB \rightarrow A + B$

[span_16](start_span)

A single reactant decomposes to form two or more products.[span_16](end_span)

  • **Thermal Decomposition** (using heat): $\text{CaCO}_3 \rightarrow \text{CaO} + \text{CO}_2$
    • [span_17](start_span)
  • **Photolytic Decomposition** (using sunlight): $2\text{AgBr} \rightarrow 2\text{Ag} + \text{Br}_2$ (Used in black & white photography).[span_17](end_span)
    • [span_18](start_span)
  • **Electrolytic Decomposition** (using electrical energy): $2\text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow 2\text{H}_2 + \text{O}_2$.[span_18](end_span)
[span_19](start_span)

*Setup for Electrolytic Decomposition of Water*[span_19](end_span)

**Test for Gases**: Hydrogen (cathode) produces a **popping sound** near a burning candle. [span_20](start_span)Oxygen (anode) makes the candle flame **burn brighter**.[span_20](end_span)

3. Displacement Reaction

  • **Single Displacement**: A more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound. [span_21](start_span)$A + BC \rightarrow AC + B$.[span_21](end_span)
    • Example: $\text{Zn} + \text{CuSO}_4 \rightarrow \text{Cu} + \text{ZnSO}_4$
  • **Double Displacement**: Exchange of ions between two reactants. $AB + CD \rightarrow AD + CB$.
    • Example: $\text{Na}_2\text{SO}_4(\text{aq}) + \text{BaCl}_2(\text{aq}) \rightarrow \text{BaSO}_4(\text{s}) + 2\text{NaCl}(\text{aq})$ (Forms a **Precipitate**).

4. Redox Reactions

    [span_22](start_span)
  • **Oxidation**: + oxygen or - hydrogen.[span_22](end_span)
    • [span_23](start_span)
  • **Reduction**: - oxygen or + hydrogen.[span_23](end_span)
    • [span_24](start_span)
  • **Oxidizing Agent**: Substance that *causes oxidation* by accepting electrons; it **gets reduced**.[span_24](end_span)
    • [span_25](start_span)
  • **Reducing Agent**: Substance that *causes reduction* by losing electrons; it **gets oxidized**.[span_25](end_span)
  • Example: $\text{ZnO} + \text{C} \rightarrow \text{Zn} + \text{CO}$
  • Example: $\text{MnO}_2 + 4\text{HCl} \rightarrow \text{MnCl}_2 + 2\text{H}_2\text{O} + \text{Cl}_2$

K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Pb > H > Cu > Hg > Ag > Au

üî• Effects of Oxidation in Daily Life (The Spoilsports)

1. Corrosion üî©

Metals are gradually destroyed by chemical reactions with substances in their environment (like moisture and acids).[span_34](end_span)

    [span_35](start_span)
  • **Examples**: Rusting of iron, Tarnishing of silver (**black coating**), Green coating on copper.[span_35](end_span)
    • [span_36](start_span)
  • **Prevention**: Coating metals with protective layers (e.g., paint or **galvanization**) to prevent exposure to oxygen and moisture.[span_36](end_span)

2. Rancidity ü•®

[span_37](start_span)

The spoilage of fats and oils in food, leading to unpleasant taste and smell.[span_37](end_span)

    [span_38](start_span)
  • **Examples**: Spoiled butter, Old cooking oil, Stale chips.[span_38](end_span)
    • [span_39](start_span)
  • **Prevention**: Adding **antioxidants**, storing foods in **airtight containers**, and **refrigerating** to slow down the oxidation process.[span_39](end_span)
