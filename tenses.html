üìñ Definition

Tense refers to the form of a verb that shows the time of action or state of being. There are three main tenses:

  • Present Tense
  • Past Tense
  • Future Tense

Each tense has four forms: Indefinite, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous. Thus, there are 12 Tenses in total.

üü¢ 1. Present Tense
(i) Simple Present (Indefinite)

Form: Subject + V1 / V1+s/es

Use: Habits, routines, universal truths.

Examples:
She goes to school.
The sun rises in the east.

(ii) Present Continuous

Form: Subject + is/are/am + V1+ing

Use: Action happening now, temporary situation.

Examples:
He is playing football.
We are going to Delhi tomorrow.

(iii) Present Perfect

Form: Subject + has/have + V3

Use: Completed action with present relevance.

Examples:
She has finished homework.
I have just eaten.

(iv) Present Perfect Continuous

Form: Subject + has/have been + V1+ing + since/for

Use: Action started in past & continuing.

Examples:
They have been studying since morning.
He has been working for 5 years.

üü° 2. Past Tense
(i) Simple Past

Form: Subject + V2

Use: Completed action in past, past habits.

Examples:
He went to school yesterday.
They played cricket.

(ii) Past Continuous

Form: Subject + was/were + V1+ing

Use: Action going on in past, interrupted action.

Examples:
She was reading at 8 pm.
They were playing when it rained.

(iii) Past Perfect

Form: Subject + had + V3

Use: Action completed before another past action.

Examples:
The train had left before we reached.
He had finished homework before dinner.

(iv) Past Perfect Continuous

Form: Subject + had been + V1+ing + since/for

Use: Action continuing before another past action.

Examples:
They had been working for 2 hours before I arrived.
She had been waiting since morning.

üî¥ 3. Future Tense
(i) Simple Future

Form: Subject + will/shall + V1

Use: Future action.

Examples:
I will help you.
She will go to college.

(ii) Future Continuous

Form: Subject + will/shall + be + V1+ing

Use: Ongoing action in future.

Examples:
They will be playing at 5 pm.
I will be waiting for you.

(iii) Future Perfect

Form: Subject + will/shall + have + V3

Use: Action completed before future time.

Examples:
She will have finished homework by 9 pm.
They will have left before you arrive.

(iv) Future Perfect Continuous

Form: Subject + will/shall + have been + V1+ing + since/for

Use: Action continuing up to a future point.

Examples:
He will have been studying for 2 hours.
They will have been living here for 10 years.
